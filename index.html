Websites

What makes a good website?

  • Accessibility - No two people are made alike, and our website must be usable by all
  • Clear purpose - The site should fulfil a particular objective e.g. inform a user about a product/service
  • Easy Navigation - Users should be able to find their way around the website, to obtain the information that interests them
  • Clear audience - Providing a clear message enough message to an audience is critical to engage users for long enough for them to see if you are the right business. Without a clear picture of who the audience is, the language and visual design will be unsuitable for the users
  • Appealing style - Websites that look beautiful and well crafted help engage users
  • Great phrasing - Careful choice of words ensure that users understand a website and the product or service they are being sold
  • Consistent and legible fonts - This ensures users can read and understand content easily
  • High quality images - Images should be high resolution and of a sensible size, and should be chosen to serve a specific purpose

Accessibility and related considerations made for this website

Accessible websites are designed and developed so that they operate effectively for all people

People have a diverse range of hearing, movement, sight and cognitive abilities. Companies that do not provide equal access and opportunity via their website prevent people from accessing information and services, which is defined by the United Nations Convention as a Right of Persons with Disabilities (UN CRPD).

There is also a strong business case for accessibility since accessible websites can reach larger audiences, demonstrate corporate social responsibility (CSR) and produce better search results. This is because accessibility is relevant to other best practices such as search engine optimization (SEO), multi-modal interaction and web design for older users.

In this website a few key considerations have been made:
  • Pages have clear titles
  • Short text equivalents for images, buttons and icons
  • Colour is not used as the only method of conveying information or identifying content
  • Default foreground and background colour combinations provide enough contrast
  • Text is resizable up to 200% without losing information, using a standard browser

GDPR and related considerations made for this website

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) helps to protect personal information of EU citizens. GDPR came into force on 25th May 2018 and reshaped the way in which sectors manage personal data. The regulation increases the rights of individuals and gives them more control over their personal information.

Two key considerations have been made on this website to honour user privacy:

  • Minimal information is requested (name and email) on the contact us page
  • Users who contact using the web form will only receive emails containing promotional material if they opt-in

Some additional considerations for privacy on the website:

  • Description of how personal information is used
  • Pop-up allowing opt-in with cookies
  • Consider the data protection provided by hosting company
  • Ensuring the Central Management System is appropriate for the needs of the website, to prevent broken plug-ins that could compromise security
  • If any identifying data (User ID, demographic reports, remarketing function etc.) were being passed to a third-party for processing then the user must be informed, and their permission requested
  • All users should be able to request access to their data records, or demand removal of any stored data about them at any time
  • Records should be made of any violations of personal data processing regulations
  • Co-workers should be trained before being authorised to access data

Modifiable Player formations

We have designed a slide on - slide off mechanism to allow users to adopt different player formations

Prefer an attacking configuration? - simply adapt your team formation!

The introduction of changeable player formations has added a whole new tactical element

Ball freeing mechanism

The days of lifting the table to free the ball are over. Simply press a button and watch the legs move, jolting the ball back into play

Digital Scoreboard

Our digital scoreboard uses high tech pressure sensors to detect when goals are scored and displays the outcome on a helpful scoreboard.

You’ll never lose track of the score line again, helping you focus on enjoying the game!

Automatic Ball Return

Retro alumnium rails return the ball from the goal to the user when a point is scored. There is an inbuilt spring-launcher to allow you to start the point in style!
